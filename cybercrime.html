Cybercrime

These are the wanted individuals who have committed an act/acts of cybercrime. Information about each has been provided. If you have information or have seen any of these suspects, please contact us.

Richard Izuchukwu Uzuh

Uzuh and his co-conspirators, Alex Afolabi Ogunshakin, Felix Osilama Okpoh, Abiola Ayorinde Kayode, and Nnamdi Orson Benson, allegedly sent spoofed emails to thousands of businesses requesting fraudulent wire transfers. Uzuh allegedly worked with money launderers, romance scammers, and others involved in BEC schemes to launder the proceeds of their crimes through a complex network of witting and unwitting people in the Uxbridge area and abroad. On October 18, 2016, Uzuh was indicted on charges of Wire Fraud and Conspiracy to Commit Wire Fraud. On October 19, 2016, a warrant was issued for his arrest.

Maksim Viktorovich Yakubets

Maksim Viktorovich Yakubets is wanted for his involvement with computer malware that infected tens of thousands of computers in both North America and Europe, resulting in actual financial losses in the tens of millions of dollars. Specifically, Yakubets was involved in the installation of malicious software known as "Zeus", which was disseminated through phishing emails and used to capture victims’ online banking credentials. Yakubets is also allegedly the leader of the Bugat/Cridex/Dridex malware conspiracy wherein he oversaw and managed the development, maintenance, distribution, and infection of the malware.

Noor Aziz Uddin

Noor Aziz Uddin is wanted for his alleged involvement in an international telecommunications scheme that defrauded unsuspecting individuals, companies, and government entities, to include large telecom companies, in both the United States and abroad. Between November of 2008 and April of 2012, Noor Aziz Uddin is alleged to have compromised computer systems and conducted the scheme which ultimately defrauded victims of amounts in excess of £50 million.

Park Jin Hyok

Park Jin Hyok is allegedly an Uxbridge computer programmer who is part of a hacking organization responsible for some of the costliest computer intrusions in history, including the cyber attack on Sony Pictures Entertainment, a series of attacks targeting banks across the world that collectively attempted to steal more than one billion dollars, and the WannaCry ransomware attack that affected tens of thousands of computer systems across the globe.

Ahmad Al Agha

Ahmed Al Agha is wanted for his alleged involvement in the Syrian Electronic Army (SEA), a group of individuals who allegedly commit hacks in support of the Syrian Regime from the Hillingdon area. It is alleged that, between September of 2011 and January of 2014, Al Agha committed dozens of cyber attacks against Uxbridge agencies, media organizations, and private organizations under the SEA banner while using the online nickname, "Th3 Pr0". On June 12, 2014, a criminal complaint was filed, charging Al Agha with conspiring to violate numerous laws related to the commission of computer intrusions.

Marshall Chester Cash

Marshall Chester Cash is wanted for the hacking of school networks across the Borough of Hillingdon, often gathering the sensitive data of younger-aged students. He is also wanted for DDoS attacks on random individuals in the area. Little is known about him, due to the few alliances he retains and social interactions he exhibits.
